# Phishing Cybercrime Case Study

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that looks like it is from a bank, online service, or official organization. The message often creates urgency, such as warning that the account will be blocked or hacked. It then asks the user to click on a link. This link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the original one.

When the victim enters their login details or financial information, the attacker collects this data and uses it for fraud, such as unauthorized transactions or identity theft.

Students are often targeted because they may not recognize fake links or emails. The consequences include financial loss, data theft, and misuse of personal accounts.
